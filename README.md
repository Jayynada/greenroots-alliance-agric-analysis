## Project Overview
This project analyzes agricultural data across 35 states in Nigeria to uncover trends in crop yield, farmer performance, investment, and revenue. The goal is to provide data-driven insights that support smarter agricultural investments and scalable growth.

## Dataset
The dataset contains farm-level records including:
- Crop type
- Investment and revenue
- Yield and farm size
- Farmer experience
- Cooperative membership
- Environmental and location data (State, LGA)

## Data Cleaning & Preparation
Key steps included:
- Removal of duplicate records
- Handling missing and inconsistent values
- Standardizing text, dates, and data types
- Correcting location errors
- Creating derived columns such as Profit, Experience Groups, Average Yield per Hectare, and Crop Duration

## Key Insights
- High-yield crops include sweet potato, sesame, rubber, cashew, and banana
- States such as Bayelsa, Borno, FCT, Gombe, and Kebbi lead in investment and revenue
- Farmers with over 16 years of experience who belong to cooperatives show higher performance and attract more investment

## Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel
- Data Cleaning & Analysis Techniques
- Descriptive Analytics

## Outcome
The analysis highlights where productivity and profitability already exist, offering a clear roadmap for targeted agricultural investment and policy decisions.
